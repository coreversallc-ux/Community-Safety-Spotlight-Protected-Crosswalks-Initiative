Community Safety Spotlight: Jonathan Hale’s Mission for Protected Crosswalks

A local citizen in Westwood, Jonathan Hale, took action on December 9, 2025, after repeated crashes and heartbreaking child fatalities at unsafe intersections. When the city failed to act quickly enough, Jonathan began painting temporary crosswalks in high‑risk areas to slow traffic and protect families.

His goal wasn’t to break the law — it was to save lives.

Why He Took Action

Westwood has seen a rising number of serious pedestrian injuries and tragic child fatalities at poorly marked or unsafe intersections. Parents pushing strollers, students walking home, and seniors crossing the street have all been put at risk by fast-moving traffic and inadequate crosswalk visibility.

After repeated community requests for safer crossings and traffic‑calming measures went unanswered, Jonathan stepped in where infrastructure failed — painting temporary crosswalks to slow cars down and protect families on their daily routes.

  • Several children in Westwood have been struck while crossing busy streets.
  • Community calls for permanent safety improvements were delayed or ignored.
  • Parents, students, and elders still face dangerous, poorly marked intersections every day.
